User Guide

Creating an Icon

A central part of using Icon Forth is creating icons to represent the words or ideas you are constructing. The images are 16x16 pixels in size and have a very restricted set of ten colors. This limited canvas is intended to provide you just enough space to uniquely represent a word. By limiting the range of colors and the size of the icon, you are encouraged to create images which are simple and thrifty.

The image editor has all you need to create simple icons. Two views of your icon are presented: normal size and zoomed. At any time, the current pen color is selected from the palette on the right, and marked with a white circle. Draw on the icon my clicking or dragging inside the zoomed view.

Describing Your Word

While an icon will allow you to recognize your word at a glance, it may be hard to locate in a sea of other icons. Additionally, people unfamiliar with your word may not fully grasp the meaning of your word. The description area is your opportunity to document and comment on your word. Use it to describe what the word does, when to use it, and possibly a short simple name by which it can be referred to verbally. A word's description is the only means by which you can later perform a text search to locate the word.

Defining Your Word

Words in Icon Forth are defined in terms of other words. The current definition of your word appears directly about the search box. To add words to the definition you must first locate them. This can be done using the gray region of the editor. Initially, a view containing words you have recently defined along with a mixture of popular words, will appear in the gray area. If you do no see the word you are looking for, enter a keyword from the word you are interested in finding, and click 'Search'. At any time you can get back to the overview list of words by click on 'All'.

You can explore a word by clicking on it. The gray region will then fill with a variety of information about the currently selected word. The word's icon will appear with a red border around it. Below that the definition of the word will appear, followed by the description text entered when the word was created. If the word is used elsewhere, a list of the most popular words containing it will appear. Information about when a word was created and last used in a definition will also appear.

Any time you see an icon, you can click on it for information about that word. The 'List' button will open a new tab or window containing the definition of the current word, followed by all other relevant definitions. The 'Run' button will execute the current word, allowing you to try it out. If you created a word and were logged in at the time, you can later delete the word with the 'Delete' button. Any words while already use your word will continue to function, but no new words will incorporate it.
